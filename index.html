About Me

I am currently a Ph.D. Candidate in Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of Iowa. I am advised by Professor Gregory H. LeFevre and part of the LeFevre Research Lab, where I am involved in discovering novel products and pathways of emerging organic contaminants in the environment to improve water quality. Previously, I completed my M.Sc. in Civil Engineering from the University of Delaware under the supervision of Professor Paul T. Imhoff. I obtained my Bachelor degree in Civil Engineering from the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET).

Research Background

I am an environmental scientist and engineer-in-training specializing in stormwater management, contaminant fate, and sustainable water reuse. With over 5 years of experience in academic research, I have designed and monitored biochar-amended stormwater systems, conducted laboratory and field-scale studies, and applied advanced analytical techniques to study contaminant behavior in diverse environmental matrices. My work aims to advance science-based solutions that improve water quality, enhance infrastructure resilience, and protect public health. I am passionate about leveraging interdisciplinary approaches to address global water challenges.
